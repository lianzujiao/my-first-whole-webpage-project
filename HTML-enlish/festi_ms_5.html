Wind blown cake

　


This is a traditional food product of the Jing nationality. This is mostly on the New Year's Eve night. The whole family is well-prepared and cooked. When the New Year arrives, it is used for the sacrifice of the ancestors, and then it is eaten with the glutinous rice porridge to show the blessing: the sweet life of the sweet person

"Wind Blowing Cake" is made by immersing the glutinous rice to make it swell, then grinding it into a thin powder, then using a spoon to smash it into a crepe with a diameter of about 40 cm (now made of thin aluminum), and the fire is steamed into a thin side. The powder film is sprinkled with some cooked sesame seeds, dried and baked on a charcoal fire. Because it is baked, it becomes thin and light, almost like a transparent film, and the wind blows up, hence the name. Its taste is crisp and refreshing, and it is a good food for hospitality and visiting relatives and friends. The "white glutinous rice cake" is also first immersed in glutinous rice, ground into a thin powder, filtered into a semi-dry with a cloth, and then kneaded into a dumpling of egg size, filled with sugar filling, boiled until it floats on the surface and is cooked soon.
