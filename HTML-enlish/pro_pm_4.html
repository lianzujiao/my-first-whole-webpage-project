Yan Xingtao

　



Yanxing Tao has a long history of pottery in Qinzhou. Its pottery art is self-contained and its local characteristics are obvious. It is one of the two treasures of Guangxi's most traditional ethnic products. It is listed as a key cultural industry in Qinzhou and even in Guangxi. Protection, promotion and development.

Yuxingtao uses the unique purple clay pottery on both sides of the Qinjiang River in Qinzhou City, Guangxi Province as the raw material, and stores the Dongmu in a closed state. After the western mud is taken back, after four to six months of sunshine and rain, it is broken, dissolved and oxidized. The weathered state, after passing through the broken soil, is mixed in a ratio of 4:6 to make a pottery blank. The east mud is soft for meat, the west mud is hard for bone, and the flesh is supported by each other and is fired by the firing technique to form the .

Qinzhou Zhaoxing ceramics works in the international exhibition held in San Francisco, Panama, in 1915, and won the gold medal. It is ranked among the four famous ceramics in China. It is well-known both at home and abroad with Yixing Zisha Pottery in Jiangsu, Jianshui Pottery in Yunnan, and Rongchang Pottery in Sichuan. In the past 100 years, it has participated in many international and national exhibitions and won more than 40 awards. The products of Yuxing Ceramics are exported to more than 30 countries and regions such as Southeast Asia, Eastern Europe, America, Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan.

In June 2008, the State Council approved the skill of the Qinzhou Yuxing pottery firing in Guangxi as a national intangible cultural heritage.
