Umi rice

　


In Wuzhou, Guangxi, eating Umi rice is a traditional custom of “March 3”. The taste of Umi rice is divided into salty and sweet. Some people say that the black-haired black rice has the effect of strengthening the stomach and warming the stomach. It is also delicious to eat.

Umi rice was originally a folk food, which was available in the Tang Dynasty. In the rural areas of Yixing, Fuyang, Jintan and Weinan in Jiangsu Province, every glutinous rice meal is on the eighth day of the fourth lunar month. Many people use homemade rice leaves to cook glutinous rice, which has become a custom. In Zhejiang, Jiangsu, Hubei, Hunan, Jiangxi, Sichuan, Guizhou, Anhui and other places, people still retain the ancient custom of eating Umi rice in the lunar calendar in April. Some places eat at the Qingming Cold Food Festival, while more are eaten on the eighth day of April. Umi rice is a unique snack of Huaxi Miao and Buyi. On the eighth day of the fourth lunar month, the Buyi people called it "The Niuwang Festival" is also called "opening the door." At the beginning of August, August 1st, the field planting began. On the day of the opening of the door, I ate the Umi rice. When I was in the field, I was strong and healthy, and I was sick. So every household should eat the fragrant Umi rice. Umi rice is a purple-black glutinous rice. It is a leaf-cooking soup that collects wild plant eucalyptus trees. It is soaked in glutinous rice with a boiled soup for half a day, then removed and steamed into a wooden chop. The glutinous rice oil is bright and fragrant, and it is cooked by glutinous rice into the black leaves for a few hours. Eating glutinous rice can detoxify the wind, prevent mosquito bites, and people believe that eating glutinous rice on the day of Lixia will protect people's peace.
