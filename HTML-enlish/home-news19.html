Guidelines for the identification of immovable cultural relics (trial) came into effect

To further standardize the identification of immovable cultural relics and provide guidance for local identification, the state administration of cultural heritage has organized and compiled the guidelines for the identification of immovable cultural relics (trial) (hereinafter referred to as the guidelines).

As required by the guidelines, local administrative departments of cultural relics at or above the county level shall conduct investigation and research, collect relevant information, fully listen to expert opinions, convene special meetings for research and make written decisions. It is necessary to confirm the noumenon and The Times, and to evaluate the value of history, art, science, society and culture.

The guidelines make it clear that the ancient ruins as the object of identification include the sites of early human activities, settlements, cities, palace and government offices, sacrificial sites, temple sites, kiln sites, mining and metallurgical sites, battlefield sites, military facilities sites, road and bridge pier sites and other types. As the object of identification, ancient tombs include tombs of emperors, tombs of celebrities or nobles, ordinary tombs and other types. As the object of identification, the ancient buildings include city wall, palace mansion, mansion, temple temple, palace ancestral hall, palace office, school house, exhibition hall, shop workshop, arch gate, temple tower, temple tower, garden garden, bridge and culvert pier, dyke and barrier, pond well spring and other types. Of identified as the object of modern modern representative buildings, including religious buildings, industrial buildings and appendages, former residence of celebrities, traditional dwellings, financial and commercial building, the old building, water conservancy facilities and appendages, culture and education buildings and appendages, health care, military construction and facilities, traffic facilities, typical buildings or other structures, large volume of various materials, such as stone, copper, iron, clay, etc.) sculpture and other types. Grotto temples and stone carvings, including grotto temples, stone carvings and rock paintings, are identified as objects. Important modern and modern historical relics, including war sites, industrial sites, major historical events and sites of important institutions, major revolutionary historical events and activities of revolutionary figures, famous tombs, martyrs' tombs and memorial facilities, etc. In addition, the remains of human activities, which are of historical, artistic and scientific value, cannot be classified into ancient sites, ancient tombs, ancient buildings, grotto temples and stone carvings, modern and modern representative buildings and important historical relics, etc., and can be identified as immovable cultural relics and classified into other categories. < / p > guidelines require that immovable cultural relics should be named in a unified manner in accordance with the "name of the county (or a conventional name)+ the name of the cultural relic body". The names of administrative villages, natural villages or corresponding small places shall be added to the categories of ancient sites and tombs.
