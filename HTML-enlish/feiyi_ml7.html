Yao folk dance "Lusheng Chang Di"

　


Fuchuan Yao Autonomous County is located in the mountainous area of ​​Wuling, the border of Hunan, Guangxi and Guangdong on the northeastern edge of Guangxi. The Fuchuan Corridor between Du Pangling and Mengzhuling has been an important passage for the Central Plains culture, Chu culture and Yao culture to enter Lingnan since ancient times. There are more than 140,000 Yao people in the county. Historical records As early as the Tang and Song Dynasties, Fuchuan was one of the main settlements of the Yao people in Guangxi. Since the Yuan, Ming and Qing Dynasties, ethnic cultures such as Yao, Han and Zhuang have infiltrated and influenced each other, thus forming an unusually rich folk dance of the Fuchuan Yao people. 　　　 Lusong Changzhao integrates the history, culture, production life, religious beliefs and literature and art of Fuchuan Pingdi Yao. It includes long drum dance, long-inspired accompaniment music, dance slogan and long drum songs, various long drum making techniques, and long-inspired entertainers. Specialized costumes, related to the myths and legends of the reeds and the folk history literature, and the large-scale folk religious ritual activities of the grazing sacrifices of the ancestors, the Yao’s history, art, literature, and religion are organically clustered around the drum dance. It constitutes the long-inspired culture of the reeds of Pingdi Yao. We know that the long-inducing culture is the core of the traditional culture of the Yao nationality and the representative cultural symbol of the Yao nationality. Fuchuan Pingdi Yao “Reed Long Dance” is an important folk dance of the Yao Nationality inspiration system in China, excavation, rescue, Protecting the “reeds and long inspirations” has great historical, cultural and scientific value.
