Dragon Festival

　


The Maonan Dragon Festival is a traditional festival in which the Maonan people pray for blessing and harvest. It is held before and after the first day of the Lunar New Year (Long Day), and the general activities are two or three days. It is the oldest, grandest and most important festival of Maonan.

Activities include praying, dragon rituals, and dragon boat racing.

"Dragon" is the rainwater. "Dragon Day" worships it before and after, and seeks its even rainfall to get a good harvest. One day before the festival, a "bull kill" a bull, with a bull's head, oxtail, cow's foot, cattle internal organs to sacrifice dragons. At the time of the sacrifice, there is a mage and a god. The beef is distributed to each household or to the market for sale, and the income is used as a holiday fee. 　　

Two or three days after the sacrifice of the dragon, each household worshipped the ancestors, the three realms, the stove king, the landlord, and so on, and also asked the gods to bless the grain. Feed the cows with risotto and steamed pork to reward the hard work of farming and give the cow a day off to let it rest. Married daughters bring their children and gifts back to their homes for the holidays, relatives and friends often go to the festival, young men and women dress up to sing and choose friends.

Nowadays, religious superstitious activities have been abandoned. Increased cultural and sports competitions or scientific activities related to production and life.
