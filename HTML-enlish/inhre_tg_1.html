Jingjiang Wangling takes the lead in digitally protecting cultural relics

2015-05-28 06:40 source:Guangxi News Net-Guangxi News 　

Guangxi News Net-Guangxi Daily Nanning News (Reporter Tang Guangsheng correspondent Fang Qiang) On May 27, the reporter learned from the Autonomous Region Surveying and Mapping Geographic Information Bureau that a few days ago, the innovative project jointly developed by Guangxi Maps Institute and Wuhan University - "Three-dimensional digitalization of cultural heritage" The key technology research on protection and display and the application in Jingling Wangling was awarded the second prize of Guangxi Science and Technology Progress by the autonomous regional government. It is reported that the project is the first time in the country to establish a three-dimensional digital information system for mausoleum sites, and to combine digital protection with archaeological research.

The project uses laser scanning, drone aerial photography and other mapping methods to establish a three-dimensional digital information system for the mausoleum site, using high-reality simulation and efficient information query technology to realistically represent cultural heritage scenes and query cultural relics information. The application of its technology marks an important step in the application of digital archiving, scientific archaeology, three-dimensional display and cultural propaganda of cultural relics in Jingjiang Wangling. It not only becomes the benchmark for the digital protection of Guangxi cultural relics, but also can be promoted to the domestic Other ruins of the Imperial Tombs and Wangling.
