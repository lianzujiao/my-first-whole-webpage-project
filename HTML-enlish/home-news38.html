The fifth China intangible cultural heritage expo traditional craft competition held the preliminary competition review meeting

The fifth China intangible cultural heritage expo, jointly sponsored by the ministry of culture and tourism and the people's government of shandong province, will open in jinan on sept 13.

On August 4, the 5th China intangible cultural heritage expo traditional craft competition preliminary competition held by the ministry of culture and tourism, the people's government of shandong province, and the people's government of jinan city was held in jinan.

The fifth China intangible cultural heritage expo is divided into several sections centering on the theme of "living inheritance and vitality reproduction", among which the traditional craft competition fully reflects the concept of "traditional craft into modern life". Six traditional craft items, including straw and willow weaving, paper cutting, clay dough making, ceramic molding, metal forging and embroidery, were arranged in the competition, which were divided into two parts, namely preliminary contest and final contest.

This competition is aimed at the inheritors of the above six categories of intangible cultural heritage projects (except the national representative inheritors) and the vast number of traditional craft practitioners, which was launched through extensive publicity by major news media, and 2,180 people registered in all parts of the country. The photos submitted by competitors from all over the country reflect the excellent skills of the competitors.

The preliminary contest has invited 30 experts from all over the country to set up a panel of evaluation experts to evaluate and grade works that meet the requirements of registration. The discipline inspectors are invited to supervise the whole review process. A total of 233 people entered the final, including 36 people in the straw and willow weaving project, 42 people in the clay dough modeling project, 34 people in the paper cutting project, 39 people in the ceramic molding project, 40 people in the metal forging project and 42 people in the embroidery project.

The final of the traditional craft competition will be held at the 5th China intangible cultural heritage expo from September 13 to 17. At that time, each contestant will have a fierce competition and present the audience with a wonderful exhibition of intangible cultural heritage production skills.
